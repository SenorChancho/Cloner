What to Eat to Keep Fine Lines at Bay

  • Raven
  • 10th Oct 2015

Don’t thwart your solid and well-developed skin care regimen with the most visible sign of aging: wrinkles. These facial creases are real showstoppers when it comes to showing off beautiful, young-looking skin.

You might say you’re still too young to be worrying about wrinkles, but actually, no one is ever too young to preventing wrinkles—the earlier you start, the better. But I don’t mean slathering your new born (to all beautiful baby mamas reading this) with copious amounts of eye cream and anti-aging serums. These aren’t the only solutions to great, young-looking skin. In fact, aging beautifully is right within arm’s reach, or realistically, a few paces to your pantry door. Here are a few yummy things you can feed your skin:



Tomatoes. 
Take a daily bite into these reds and your skin will thank you for it. These are Mother Nature’s gift to women contains a handful of collagen to encourage skin elasticity. They are also rich in Vitamin C and lycopene—strong antioxidants that help improve the circulation of oxygen to the skin.

Soy
Soy has been at the top of the beauty loop for quite some time now, ad for good reason. Go ahead and load up on these silky grubs because studies show that the isoflavone in soy can actually help you prevent deep lines and sagging. It does so by boosting the collagen occurring naturally in your body and prevents it from breaking down.

Yogurt. 
Proteins are known to resist wrinkles. And these healthy treats are loaded with it. Include this in your daily diet (if you don’t already) to delay these pesky skin creases and crow’s feet.

Pomegranates.
Do it like Persephone and dig into these beautiful pink fruits to keep sallowness at bay! Eat them, drink them—it doesn’t matter as long as you intake them. Or rub them all over your face, if you want. You can minimize the appearance of fine lines by slathering these all over your skin.

Olive Oil. 
This gourmet staple is healthy and has effective anti-aging qualities too. This oil is packed with monounsaturated fatty acids and polyphenol antioxidants. Okay, that’s a mouthful. What we mean is, in simple terms: it can make your skin look younger—and help you shed pounds too!
